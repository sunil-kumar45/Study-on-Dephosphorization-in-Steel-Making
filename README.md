# 🔍 Project Overview

Phosphorus is one of the most detrimental impurity elements in steel. Its presence adversely affects ductility, toughness, and weldability, especially at low temperatures.

This project focuses on understanding:

Mechanism of Dephosphorization in BOF (Basic Oxygen Furnace)

Role of slag chemistry and basicity

Thermodynamics and kinetics of phosphorus removal

Effect of oxygen blowing parameters

Industrial practices followed at RINL–VSP Steel Melt Shop

Analysis of plant data related to phosphorus control

# ⚙️ Technical Concepts Covered

Basic Oxygen Steelmaking (LD Converter)

Slag–Metal reactions

Thermodynamics of phosphorus oxidation

Ellingham diagram significance

Role of CaO, MgO and slag basicity

Oxygen potential control

Quality monitoring by QA & TD Department

Continuous Casting considerations
